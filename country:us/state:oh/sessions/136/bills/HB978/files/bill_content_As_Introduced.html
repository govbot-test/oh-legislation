To amend sections 109.572, 503.41, 3767.10, and 5101.87 and to enact sections 109.45, 109.451, 109.452, 109.453, 109.454, 109.455, 2905.34, 3724.01, 3724.02, 3724.03, 3724.04, 3724.05, 3724.06, 3724.07, 3724.99, and 5502.71 of the Revised Code regarding the licensure and regulation of massage businesses and to make an appropriation.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF OHIO:

Section 1. That sections 109.572, 503.41, 3767.10, and 5101.87 be amended and sections 109.45, 109.451, 109.452, 109.453, 109.454, 109.455, 2905.34, 3724.01, 3724.02, 3724.03, 3724.04, 3724.05, 3724.06, 3724.07, 3724.99, and 5502.71 of the Revised Code be enacted to read as follows:

Sec. 109.45. As used in sections 109.45 to 109.455 of the Revised Code, "massage operations" has the same meaning as in section 2905.34 of the Revised Code.

Sec. 109.451. A person conducting massage operations shall use a financial transaction device to receive payment for all services provided by the person. As used in this section, "financial transaction device" has the same meaning as in section 113.40 of the Revised Code.

Sec. 109.452. A person conducting massage operations shall report to the attorney general the payroll of all employees and shall cross-reference in the report the license number for each employee licensed under sections 503.40 to 503.50 or 715.61 of the Revised Code or Chapter 3724. of the Revised Code.

Sec. 109.453. No person conducting massage operations shall classify an employee of the person as an independent contractor.

Sec. 109.454. The illicit massage task force is created in the office of the attorney general to monitor suspicious transactions and to investigate and correct erroneous employee classification as an independent contractor regarding massage operations.

Sec. 109.455. The attorney general shall adopt rules under Chapter 119. of the Revised Code governing the requirements of sections 109.45 to 109.455 of the Revised Code, including rules that do the following:

(A) Establish the method and manner of reporting employee payroll under section 109.452 of the Revised Code;

(B) Guide persons conducting massage operations in designating individuals as employees or independent contractors for purposes of sections 109.452 and 109.453 of the Revised Code;

(C) Establish standards for persons conducting massage operations as to what transactions should be considered suspicious for purposes of section 109.454 of the Revised Code;

(D) Provide guidance as to the operations, activities, and duties of the illicit massage task force under section 109.454 of the Revised Code.

Sec. 503.41. (A) A board of township trustees, by resolution, may regulate and require the registration of massage establishments and their employees within the unincorporated territory of the township. In accordance with sections 503.40 to 503.49 of the Revised Code, for that purpose, the board, by a majority vote of all members, may adopt, amend, administer, and enforce regulations within the unincorporated territory of the township, including regulations to do the following:

(1) Limit operable hours for massage establishments;

(2) Require distance buffers from hotels and other high trafficking corridors;

(3) Require visible lobby areas and surveillance retention;

(4) Prohibit residential occupancy inside massage establishments.

(B) A board may adopt regulations and amendments under this section only after public hearing at not fewer than two regular sessions of the board. The board shall publish notice of the public hearings, including the time, date, and place, once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the hearings using at least one of the following methods:

(1) In the print or digital edition of a newspaper of general circulation in the township;

(2) On the official public notice web site established under section 125.182 of the Revised Code;

(3) On the web site and social media account of the township.

The board shall make available proposed regulations or amendments to the public at the office of the board.

(C) Regulations or amendments adopted by the board are effective thirty days after the date of adoption unless, within thirty days after the adoption of the regulations or amendments, the township fiscal officer receives a petition, signed by a number of qualified electors residing in the unincorporated area of the township equal to not less than ten per cent of the total vote cast for all candidates for governor in the area at the most recent general election at which a governor was elected, requesting the board to submit the regulations or amendments to the electors of the area for approval or rejection at the next primary or general election occurring at least ninety days after the board receives the petition.

No regulation or amendment for which the referendum vote has been requested is effective unless a majority of the votes cast on the issue is in favor of the regulation or amendment. Upon certification by the board of elections that a majority of the votes cast on the issue was in favor of the regulation or amendment, the regulation or amendment takes immediate effect.

(D) The board shall make available regulations it adopts or amends to the public at the office of the board and shall cause to be published once a notice of the availability of the regulations within ten days after their adoption or amendment, using at least one of the following methods:

(1) In the print or digital edition of a newspaper of general circulation in the township;

(2) On the official public notice web site established under section 125.182 of the Revised Code;

(3) On the web site and social media account of the township.

(E) Nothing in sections 503.40 to 503.49 of the Revised Code shall be construed to allow a board of township trustees toregulate to regulate the practice of any limited branch of medicine specified in section 4731.15 of the Revised Code or the practice of providing therapeutic massage by a licensed physician, a licensed chiropractor, a licensed podiatrist, a licensed nurse, or any other licensed health professional. As used in this division, "licensed" means licensed, certified, or registered to practice in this state.

(F) If a township adopts regulations to require the registration of massage establishments and their employees, the township shall comply with Chapter 4796. of the Revised Code.

Sec. 2905.34. (A) As used in this section, "massage operations" means activities conducted by the following:

(1) A massage business, as that term is defined in section 3724.01 of the Revised Code and that is subject to the requirements of sections 3724.01 to 3724.07 of the Revised Code;

(2) A massage professional subject to licensure under section 715.61 of the Revised Code;

(3) A massage establishment, as that term is defined in section 503.40 of the Revised Code, that is subject to the requirements of sections 503.40 to 503.50 of the Revised Code;

(4) A person conducting activities for which licensure is required, but not obtained, under sections 503.40 to 503.50 of the Revised Code, section 715.61 of the Revised Code, or sections 3724.01 to 3724.07 of the Revised Code.

(B) No person, knowing that property is the proceeds of a violation of section 2905.32 of the Revised Code involving massage operations or a monetary instrument given, received, or intended to be used in support of a violation of section 2905.32 of the Revised Code involving massage operations, shall knowingly conduct or attempt to conduct any transaction involving that property or knowingly transport, transmit, or transfer that monetary instrument with the intent to do any of the following:

(1) Commit or further the commission of criminal activity;

(2) Conceal or disguise the nature, location, source, ownership, or control of either the proceeds of a violation of section 2905.32 of the Revised Code involving massage operations or a monetary instrument given, received, or intended to be used to support a violation of section 2905.32 of the Revised Code involving massage operations;

(3) Conceal or disguise the intent to avoid the reporting requirement under section 109.451 of the Revised Code.

(C) Whoever violates this section is guilty of money laundering through massage operations, a felony of the third degree.

Sec. 3724.01. As used in this chapter:

(A) "Massage business" means a commercial enterprise where a person offers massage therapy in exchange for anything of value.

(B) "Massage therapist" means an individual licensed under Chapter 4731. of the Revised Code to practice massage therapy.

(C) "Massage therapy" has the same meaning as in section 4731.04 of the Revised Code.

Sec. 3724.02. (A) No person shall operate a massage business without holding a license issued by the director of health under this section.

(B) Every person that proposes to operate a massage business shall apply to the director of health for a license. Application shall be made on forms prescribed by the director, shall include such information as the director requires, and shall be accompanied by the license fee established by rules of the director of health adopted under section 3724.03 of the Revised Code. The application shall include the report of each criminal records check requested pursuant to division (D) of this section.

The director shall grant a license to the applicant if the applicant is in compliance with this chapter and rules adopted under it.

(C) A license granted under this section shall be valid for two years unless revoked or suspended. Application for renewal of a license shall be made in the same manner as for an initial license and shall be accompanied by the renewal fee established by rules of the director of health adopted under section 3724.03 of the Revised Code.

(D) Each person applying for a massage business license under this section shall request that the superintendent of the bureau of criminal identification and investigation conduct a criminal records check of each owner of the massage business in the same manner as a criminal records check is requested for an applicant pursuant to section 3724.05 of the Revised Code.

Sec. 3724.03. (A) In accordance with Chapter 119. of the Revised Code, the director of health shall adopt, and may amend and rescind, rules that do both of the following:

(1) Create application procedures for massage business licenses pursuant to section 3724.02;

(2) Establish fees for massage business licenses pursuant to section 3724.02.

(B) The director may adopt any other rules as necessary to implement this chapter, including rules consistent with section 9.79 of the Revised Code that specify offenses for which a conviction or guilty plea require a massage business to deny employment to an applicant pursuant to section 3724.05 of the Revised Code.

Sec. 3724.04. Each massage business shall do both of the following:

(A) Provide each employee, at the time of hire, with a copy of the notices required to be posted under sections 4111.09, 4112.07, 4123.54, and 4123.83 of the Revised Code in the employee's native language;

(B) Display both of the following in a publicly visible location:

(1) The contact information for an anti-trafficking hotline;

(2) Proof that each individual employed by the massage business to perform massage therapy is licensed as a massage therapist under Chapter 4731. of the Revised Code.

Sec. 3724.05. (A) As used in this section:

(1) "Applicant" means a person who is under final consideration for employment at a licensed massage business.

(2) "Criminal records check" has the same meaning as in section 109.572 of the Revised Code.

(B)(1) The owner of a massage business shall request that the superintendent of the bureau of criminal identification and investigation conduct a criminal records check of each applicant. If an applicant for whom a criminal records check request is required under this division does not present proof of having been a resident of this state for the five-year period immediately prior to the date the criminal records check is requested or provide evidence that within that five-year period the superintendent has requested information about the applicant from the federal bureau of investigation in a criminal records check, the owner shall request that the superintendent obtain information from the federal bureau of investigation as part of the criminal records check of the applicant. Even if an applicant for whom a criminal records check request is required under this division presents proof of having been a resident of this state for the five-year period, the owner may request that the superintendent include information from the federal bureau of investigation in the criminal records check.

(2) A person required by division (B)(1) of this section to request a criminal records check shall do both of the following:

(a) Provide to each applicant for whom a criminal records check request is required under that division a copy of the form prescribed pursuant to division (C)(1) of section 109.572 of the Revised Code and a standard fingerprint impression sheet prescribed pursuant to division (C)(2) of that section, and obtain the completed form and impression sheet from the applicant;

(b) Forward the completed form and impression sheet to the superintendent of the bureau of criminal identification and investigation.

(3) An applicant provided the form and fingerprint impression sheet under division (B)(2)(a) of this section who fails to complete the form or provide fingerprint impressions shall not be employed by the massage business.

(C) No massage business shall employ a person who has been convicted of or pleaded guilty to an offense specified by rules of the director of health adopted under section 3724.03 of the Revised Code.

(D)(1) Each massage business shall pay to the bureau of criminal identification and investigation the fee prescribed pursuant to division (C)(3) of section 109.572 of the Revised Code for each criminal records check conducted pursuant to a request made under division (B) of this section.

(2) A massage business may charge an applicant a fee not exceeding the amount the program pays under division (D)(1) of this section. A massage business may collect a fee only if the program notifies the person at the time of initial application for employment of the amount of the fee and that, unless the fee is paid, the person will not be considered for employment.

(E) The report of a criminal records check conducted pursuant to a request made under this section is not a public record for the purposes of section 149.43 of the Revised Code and shall not be made available to any person other than the following:

(1) The individual who is the subject of the criminal records check or the individual's representative;

(2) The owner of the massage business requesting the criminal records check or the owner's representative;

(3) A court, hearing officer, or other necessary individual involved in a case dealing with a denial of employment of the applicant or dealing with employment or unemployment benefits of the applicant;

(4) The director of health.

(F) The owner of a massage business shall inform each individual, at the time of initial application, that the individual is required to provide a set of fingerprint impressions and that a criminal records check is required to be conducted if the individual comes under final consideration for employment.

(G) The owner of a massage business shall submit the report of each criminal records check conducted pursuant to this section to the director of health within ten calendar days of hiring a new employee.

Sec. 3724.06. Each owner of a licensed massage business shall do both of the following every two years:

(A) Request that the superintendent of the bureau of criminal identification and investigation conduct a criminal records check on each owner of the massage business and each employee of the massage business in the same manner as a criminal records check is conducted for an applicant pursuant to section 3724.05 of the Revised Code;

(B) Submit the report of each criminal records check conducted pursuant to this section to the director of health.

Sec. 3724.07. (A) At least once annually, the director of health shall inspect each licensed massage business.

(B) The director may at any time inspect a licensed massage business to address an incident that may impact public health and safety, respond to a complaint submitted to the director, or otherwise ensure the massage business is in compliance with this chapter and other state and federal laws.

(1) Employing individuals to provide massage therapy who are not licensed to practice massage therapy under Chapter 4731. of the Revised Code;

(2) Maintaining locked sleeping quarters;

(3) Receiving only cash payments without maintaining accurate payroll records;

(4) Refusing inspection.

Sec. 3767.10. (A) If a tenant or occupant of a building or tenement, under a lawful title, uses such place for the purposes of lewdness, assignation, or prostitution, such use makes void the lease or other title under which he the tenant or occupant holds, at the option of the owner, and, without any act of the owner, causes the right of possession to revert and vest in such owner, who may without process of law make immediate entry upon the premises.

(B) If a building or tenement is utilized for the purposes of lewdness, assignation, or prostitution on an ongoing or recurrent basis, the attorney general may file an expedited civil action to prohibit the operation of any business in the building by a tenant or occupant who is the alleged perpetrator of the lewdness, assignation, or prostitution. Where the property owner knows or reasonably should have known about the ongoing or recurrent lewdness, assignation, or prostitution, the court shall impose civil penalties upon the building owner. The court shall transmit a copy of the final order in a civil case under this section against a property owner to the tax commissioner.

(C) If the court imposes a civil penalty upon a building owner under division (B) of this section, the parcel encompassing the building or tenement utilized for the purposes of lewdness, assignation, or prostitution shall be ineligible for any real property tax reduction, credit, or exemption for the tax year that includes the date of the final order and either the following tax year or through the tax year that includes the date that the business violating division (A) of this section vacates the premises, whichever is sooner. The tax commissioner shall notify the county auditor of any such ineligible property.

Sec. 5101.87. (A) There is hereby created in the treasury of state the victims of human trafficking fund consisting of money seized in connection with a violation of section 2905.32, 2907.21, or 2907.22 of the Revised Code or acquired from the sale of personal effects, tools, or other property seized because the personal effects, tools, or other property were used in the commission of a violation of section 2905.32, 2907.21, or 2907.22 of the Revised Code or derived from the proceeds of the commission of a violation of section 2905.32, 2907.21, or 2907.22 of the Revised Code and deposited pursuant to section 2981.12 of the Revised Code and such other money as may be appropriated or contributed to the fund.

(B) Money in the fund shall be used for the sole purpose of treating, caring for, rehabilitating, educating, housing, and providing assistance for victims of trafficking in persons, including legal aid and relocation services. The office of the attorney general shall administer the fund.
