Section 1:
As Offered 136th General Assembly Regular Session H. R. No. 175 2025-2026 Representative Deeter A R E S O L U T I O N Honoring Ariannah Floyd as a 2025 Division II State Track and Field Champion. WHEREAS, The members of the House of Representatives of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio are pleased to congratulate Ariannah Floyd on winning the 2025 Division II State Championship in the 300-meter hurdles; and WHEREAS, A senior at Firelands High School, Ariannah Floyd has combined talent and competitive spirit with the highest ideals of good sportsmanship to establish herself as a truly exceptional young athlete. With admirable dedication, she has worked diligently toward attaining ultimate proficiency in track and field, and as a result, she placed first in the 300-meter hurdles with a time of 42.76 seconds at this year’s Division II State Meet; and WHEREAS, Ariannah Floyd’s accomplishments and leadership, both in and out of competition, are a justifiable source of pride and a fine reflection not only on her but also on her family, her teammates, her coaches, her school, and her community. Her unwavering commitment to excellence has earned her the respect and esteem of many, and she is certainly worthy of high praise; and WHEREAS, With athletic competition playing such an important role in the total education of our young people, we are extremely pleased to pay tribute to Ariannah Floyd on an exemplary season. Indeed, the same maturity and experience she has gained through her participation in track and field will greatly aid her in meeting the challenges of the future; therefore be it RESOLVED, That we, the members of the House of Representatives of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio, in adopting this Resolution, commend Ariannah Floyd as the 2025 Division II State Track and Field Champion in the 300-meter hurdles and extend best wishes for continued success; and be it further RESOLVED, That the Clerk of the House of Representatives transmit a duly authenticated copy of this Resolution to Ariannah Floyd.
