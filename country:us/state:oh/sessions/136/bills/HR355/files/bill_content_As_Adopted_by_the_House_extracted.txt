Section 1:
As Adopted by the House 136th General Assembly Regular Session H. R. No. 355 2025-2026 Representatives White, A., Lampton A R E S O L U T I O N Honoring Brooklyn Talley as a 2026 Division II State Swimming Champion. WHEREAS, The members of the House of Representatives of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio are pleased to congratulate Brooklyn Talley on placing first in the 100‑yard freestyle at the 2026 Division II State Meet; and WHEREAS, A freshman at Archbishop Carroll High School, Brooklyn Talley has combined talent and competitive spirit with the highest ideals of good sportsmanship to establish herself as a truly exceptional young athlete. With admirable dedication, she has worked diligently toward attaining ultimate proficiency in swimming, and as a result, she won first place in the 100-yard freestyle with a time of 51.12 and second place in the 200-yard freestyle with a time of 1:50.83 at this year’s Division II State Meet; and WHEREAS, Brooklyn Talley’s accomplishments and leadership, both in and out of competition, are a justifiable source of pride and a fine reflection not only on her but also on her family, her teammates, her coaches, and her Patriots school community. Her unwavering commitment to excellence has earned her the respect and esteem of many, and she is certainly worthy of high praise; and WHEREAS, With athletic competition playing such an important role in the total education of our young people, we are extremely pleased to pay tribute to Brooklyn Talley on an exemplary season. Indeed, the same maturity and experience she has gained through her participation in swimming will greatly aid her in meeting the challenges of the future; therefore be it RESOLVED, That we, the members of the House of Representatives of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio, in adopting this Resolution, commend Brooklyn Talley as a 2026 Division II State Swimming Champion and extend best wishes for continued success; and be it further RESOLVED, That the Clerk of the House of Representatives transmit a duly authenticated copy of this Resolution to Brooklyn Talley.
