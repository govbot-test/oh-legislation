A R E S O L U T I O N

Recognizing Multiple System Atrophy Awareness Month in Ohio.

WHEREAS, The members of the House of Representatives of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio are pleased to recognize March 2025 as Multiple System Atrophy Awareness Month; and

WHEREAS, Multiple System Atrophy Awareness Month provides a unique opportunity to highlight an issue of great importance to our state. Formerly known as Shy-Drager Syndrome, MSA is a rare and fatal neurodegenerative disorder with no known cure, and its various symptoms, which include impairments to balance, difficulty with movement, poor coordination, bladder dysfunction, sleep disturbance, and poor blood pressure control, can have a tremendous impact on a person’s quality of life; and

WHEREAS, Incidents of MSA are often misdiagnosed as other neurodegenerative diseases due to an overlapping of symptoms, and the obscure nature of this condition has left many patients struggling to find relief. In an effort to overcome these challenges, Multiple System Atrophy Awareness Month was designated by the 129th General Assembly of Ohio in 2013, and in the twelve years since, it has helped to increase public understanding of this disorder among the residents of Ohio; and

WHEREAS, It is through the work of caring and conscientious individuals such as all those associated with the observance of Multiple System Atrophy Awareness Month that our state has gained a reputation for the diligence and concern of its citizens. Indeed, we applaud the many people who have played a role in bringing attention to this progressive neurological disease; therefore be it

RESOLVED, That we, the members of the House of Representatives of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio, in adopting this Resolution, recognize March 2025 as Multiple System Atrophy Awareness Month; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the Clerk of the House of Representatives transmit duly authenticated copies of this Resolution to the news media of Ohio.
