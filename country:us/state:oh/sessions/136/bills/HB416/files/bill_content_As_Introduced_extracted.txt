As Introduced 136th General Assembly Regular Session	H. B. No. 416 2025-2026 Representatives Miller, J., Manning Cosponsors: Representatives Abrams, Baker, Barhorst, Bird, Brennan, Brent, Brownlee, Click, Cockley, Craig, Daniels, Dean, Deeter, Demetriou, Dovilla, Fischer, Fowler Arthur, Ghanbari, Glassburn, Grim, Gross, Hall, T., Hall, D., Hiner, Holmes, Isaacsohn, Jarrells, John, Johnson, King, Klopfenstein, Lampton, LaRe, Lear, Lett, Lorenz, Mathews, A., Mathews, T., McClain, McNally, Miller, K., Miller, M., Mullins, Newman, Odioso, Oelslager, Peterson, Piccolantonio, Pizzulli, Plummer, Rader, Ray, Richardson, Robb Blasdel, Robinson, Russo, Salvo, Santucci, Schmidt, Sigrist, Sims, Somani, Stephens, Swearingen, Synenberg, Thomas, C., Thomas, D., Thomas, J., Troy, Upchurch, White, A., White, E., Willis, Workman, Young To enact section 5534.448 of the Revised Code to designate a portion of United States Route 6 in Lorain County as the "Police Officer Phillip Wagner Memorial Highway." BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF OHIO: Section 1. That section 5534.448 of the Revised Code be enacted to read as follows: Sec. 5534.448. In addition to any other name prescribed in the Revised Code or otherwise, the eastbound and westbound lanes of United States route number six between state route number fifty-eight and state route number fifty-seven, within the municipal corporation of Lorain, in Lorain county, shall be known as the "Police Officer Phillip Wagner Memorial Highway." The director of transportation may erect suitable markers along the highway indicating its name.
