As Introduced 136th General Assembly Regular Session	H. C. R. No. 33 2025-2026 Representatives Russo, Grim Cosponsors: Representatives Jarrells, Troy, Synenberg, Brennan, McNally, Upchurch A c o n c u r r e n t R E S O L U T I O N Honoring the longstanding partnership between the Province of Québec and the State of Ohio. BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF OHIO (THE SENATE CONCURRING): WHEREAS, Québec maintains close ties with the State of Ohio, notably through their involvement in the Conference of the Great Lakes and St. Lawrence Governors and Premiers, and the Great Lakes-St. Lawrence Legislative Caucus; and WHEREAS, Québec and the State of Ohio, as well as by the provinces and cities involved in the Great Lakes-St. Lawrence Conference, the Great Lakes-St. Lawrence Legislative Caucus, and the Great Lakes and St. Lawrence Cities Initiatives attach importance to issues relating to water, trade and the environment; and WHEREAS, The Great Lakes and St. Lawrence states and provinces manage together 20% of the world's surface fresh water and provide drinking water for over 40 million people, emphasizing the importance of their relationship and their strategic position in terms of freshwater management and sustainability; and WHEREAS, The Great Lakes and St. Lawrence states and provinces together constitute an economic region equivalent to the world's third largest economy; and WHEREAS, Québec and Ohio both strive to support and improve tourism in the Great Lakes-St. Lawrence region and to promote sustainable tourism; and WHEREAS, Over the past five years, trade between Québec and Ohio has increased by nearly 20%; and WHEREAS, Québec is Ohio's second largest investor among Canadian provinces and ranks as the sixth largest trading partner of Ohio; and WHEREAS, In 2023, Québec was a critical supplier for Ohio's aerospace and transport sector, enhancing the state's technological innovation and global competitiveness; and WHEREAS, Québec was the United States' principal partner in primary aluminum in 2023; and WHEREAS, Québec has significantly contributed to Ohio's transportation and automotive sectors, ranking second among international importers of Ohio-made trucks, tractors, diesel engines, spark ignition and piston engines, and ranking third for motor cars, trailers, and parts for motor vehicles; and WHEREAS, Nearly 25 Québec-owned companies currently operate in Ohio while more than 70 Ohio-based businesses maintain operations in Québec, directly creating jobs and stimulating local economies; and WHEREAS, Québec holds recognized expertise in quantum computing, a field of emerging importance that aligns with Ohio's own interests in developing advanced technology industries; and WHEREAS, Both Québec and Ohio maintain expertise and leadership in the aerospace industry with partnerships among many significant institutions and companies; now therefore be it RESOLVED, That the members of this body honor the decades-long partnership between the Province of Québec and the State of Ohio and reaffirm its commitment to the strengthening and deepening of its sister-state relationship; and be it further RESOLVED, That the Clerk of the House of Representatives transmit a duly authenticated copy of this Resolution to the news media of Ohio and to the National Assembly of Québec.
