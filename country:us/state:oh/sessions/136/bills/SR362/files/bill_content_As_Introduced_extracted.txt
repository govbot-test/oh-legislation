sr362_00_IN As Introduced 136th General Assembly Regular Session S. R. No. 362 2025-2026 Senators Manning, McColley Cosponsors: Senators Antonio, Blackshear, Blessing, Brenner, Chavez, Cirino, Craig, Cutrona, DeMora, Gavarone, Hicks-Hudson, Huffman, Ingram, Johnson, Koehler, Landis, Lang, Liston, Manchester, O'Brien, Patton, Reineke, Reynolds, Roegner, Romanchuk, Schaffer, Smith, Timken, Weinstein, Wilkin, Wilson A R E S O L U T I O N In memory of Jeffry Armbruster. WHEREAS, The members of the Senate of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio extend our sincere condolences to the family and friends of Jeffry Armbruster on the sorrowful occasion of his death; and WHEREAS, Jeff Armbruster will be remembered fondly by all those who knew and loved him, including his wife, Lee, his six children, and his circle of family and friends, and his absence will leave an irreparable void in our society. Respected for his myriad civic, professional, and personal endeavors, he was celebrated for his integrity, and he brought a wealth of knowledge and a heart for the public welfare into all of his efforts to benefit the State of Ohio, Lorain County, and the City of North Ridgeville; and WHEREAS, Jeff Armbruster served as the mayor of North Ridgeville from 1989 until 1995 and as a member of the Ohio Senate from 1999 until 2006, where he represented the people of the 13th District and held such positions as the chair of the Highways and Transportation and Ohio Turnpike Oversight committees and the vice chair of the Insurance, Commerce, and Labor and Health, Human Services, and Aging committees, and he garnered the respect of his peers as well as his constituents. Clearly, his stalwart legacy to our state will endure as a testament to his passion for community service as well as his positive philosophy for public life; and WHEREAS, Jeff Armbruster made a lasting impression through his leadership, and in his various roles as the owner of Pinzone Armbruster and Armbruster Energy Enterprises, the safety service director for the City of North Ridgeville, and the administrator for Lorain County, he exemplified the belief that the power of local leaders to enrich and strengthen the communities they represent would be best achieved by working together. Indeed, he will be remembered for his visionary example of citizenship and his support of the State of Ohio; therefore be it RESOLVED, That we, the members of the Senate of the 136th General Assembly of Ohio, in adopting this Resolution, offer our heartfelt condolences to the family and friends of Jeffry Armbruster and express a profound sense of loss on the sorrowful occasion of the death of a fine Ohioan; and be it further RESOLVED, That the Clerk of the Senate transmit a duly authenticated copy of this Resolution to the family of Jeffry Armbruster.
